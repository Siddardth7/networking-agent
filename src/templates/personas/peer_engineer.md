The recipient is a **Peer Engineer or Individual Contributor** (engineer, analyst, specialist) at the target company — usually 1–5 years ahead of the sender. The goal is genuine peer connection that can grow into mentorship, an honest look at the team, and eventually a referral.

Follow the 4-Part Message Model (Intro → Source → Hook → Close). For peers:

- **Source** — position the sender as someone walking a path they're a few steps ahead on. "I'm exploring people working in {domain} as I finish my degree" or "looking to connect with engineers who've moved into {field} and came across your profile". Honest and slightly deferential — the sender wants to learn from them.
- **Hook** — show real curiosity about THEIR work. Ask (implicitly) about their day-to-day, how they tackled a specific project, or congratulate a real, recent achievement (promotion, paper, project). The hook must be specific to them, pulled from their profile — never generic. One light credibility clause about the sender's own background is fine, but lead with interest in them, not a pitch.
- **Close (one soft ask — and ROTATE it across peers)** — a single, low-pressure invitation to talk shop: "Would value connecting and hearing what the work on {team/area} is actually like" or "Grateful to connect and swap notes on {domain}". Aim for a short chat / coffee, not a hard pitch. When reaching several peers at the same company, VARY which angle you ask about so the conversations don't read as one copied script — rotate among: what the day-to-day work is actually like; how they approached a specific project/challenge; what the team culture and trajectory feel like; how they broke into the field; what they'd tell someone finishing a degree aiming at this work. One ask each, varied across people.

Tone:
- Casual and collegial — engineer-to-engineer, contractions on.
- Don't pretend to know their job; ask, don't assert.
- Never sound like a cover letter. This is a conversation starter.
- Connection note ≤280 chars; email ~110 words; keep post-connection messages conversational.
