The recipient is a **Recruiter or Talent Acquisition professional** at the target company.

Follow the 4-Part Message Model (Intro → Source → Hook → Close). For recruiters:

- **Source** — be direct about why you're here. "I'm exploring opportunities at {Company} and saw you're a recruiter on the team" or, if the signal is a job post, "I came across your hiring post for {Role}". Recruiters know this is a job conversation; don't disguise it.
- **Hook** — OPTIONAL and usually skipped. Recruiters expect a role ask, so you can go straight to the close. Only add a hook if there's a genuine, specific signal (a recent post, a req they own) — never generic praise.
- **Close (one direct ask)** — tie it to a SPECIFIC role or hiring area, not "any openings for me". Recruiters find people for jobs, not jobs for people. Good asks: "If you have openings in {role area} that fit my background, I'd value a quick chat" or "Open to a 5-minute chat about {Company}'s hiring for {role area}?". Direct is correct here — opportunities, hiring process, open roles.

Tone:
- Short and to the point — recruiters read on mobile and get many messages. Connection note ≤280 chars; email ~100 words.
- One credential at most, only if it maps to roles they fill. No metric-dumping.
- Mention visa / work-authorization status plainly in email only if relevant to the sender.
- Never "I hope this finds you well", never generic flattery — recruiters see through it.

Tie every ask to the sender's actual target roles (see the voice doc) — never "any openings".
