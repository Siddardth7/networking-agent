## Tone
# [CUSTOMIZE] Describe how YOU talk. The defaults below favor a conversational,
# context-first, specificity-driven voice.
- Conversational, not corporate — write the way a sharp engineer actually talks.
  Use contractions (I'm, you're, I've). Never sound like a cover letter.
- Context-first opener: it is good and honest to say *why* you're reaching out
  and *how* you found them. Do NOT strip this out in favor of a cold fact.
- Specificity is everything. The one thing that separates a real note from a
  template is a detail taken directly from THEIR profile. If you can't name a
  specific thing, the message is too generic — shorten it, don't fake it.
- Confident without overselling. Never say how "passionate" or "motivated" you are.
- Never beg, never apologize for reaching out, never hedge. One clear,
  low-pressure ask they can answer in one reply.

## The 4-Part Message Model
# This is the spine of every message. Channel length decides how many parts
# survive; the ORDER never changes. Persona templates specialize parts 2-4.

1. **Intro (greeting).** "Hi {first name}," for LinkedIn. Email may add a brief
   human opener.
2. **Source (how/why you found them).** Honest context for the outreach,
   persona-specific. Allowed and encouraged — not filler.
3. **Hook (the specific, personalized detail).** The most important part. One
   real, specific thing from their profile and why it resonates. SPECIFICITY
   GATE: must name a specific thing, or be omitted. Never fabricate.
4. **Close (one ask).** Exactly ONE low-friction ask, persona-specific type.
   Never stack a second ask.

## Specificity Gate (hard rule)
A "Source" or "Hook" line that names no specific person/company detail is a
defect. Generic admiration ("impressive work") reads as filler and lowers reply
rates. When the only specific thing is company-generic, prefer a shorter message.

## LinkedIn Connection Note Rules
# LinkedIn caps notes at 300 characters on all plans (free accounts are limited
# on note *count*, not length). We enforce a safe 280-char cutoff in code;
# over-280 notes are HARD_FAIL.
- You have room for most of the 4-part model: Intro + Source + Hook + a short
  Close usually fits in 280. Recruiters may drop the Hook; alumni merge
  Source+Hook.
- Don't pad to fill space. A generic "exploring roles in {field}" preamble adds
  nothing — keep the Source honest and tied to them, make the Hook specific. A
  brief self-identity ("MS AE at UIUC, composites") is fine; don't let it crowd
  out the Hook.
- Never ask for a call in the note — it earns the reply, not the meeting.
- No hashtags, no emojis, no exclamation marks.
- Target 220-270 characters; leave headroom under the 280 cutoff.

## LinkedIn Post-Connection Rules
# Sent after the connection is accepted. Full 4-part model unfolds here.
- One short paragraph, 3-5 sentences.
- Briefly re-anchor who you are (one clause).
- One real, specific Hook; if you lack a specific signal, OMIT it — never emit a
  placeholder like [RESEARCH_NEEDED] (the guardrail blocks it).
- One persona-appropriate ask, no apology.
- Do not attach a resume; offer to share if they ask.

## Cold Email Rules
# [CUSTOMIZE] Adjust word limits and CTA style to match your preference.
- Subject: 6 words or fewer; specific, not clever. Avoid clickbait.
- Body 90-150 words; never exceed 175. Full 4-part model, prose only.
- Para 1 (Intro+Source) / Para 2 (Hook) / Para 3 (Close — one ask, not three).
- No bullet points. No attachments in first contact.
