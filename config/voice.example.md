## Tone
- Professional but conversational — write like a sharp engineer, not a recruiter
- Direct: lead with the most relevant fact, not a warm-up sentence
- Curious and specific: reference something real about the person or company,
  not generic flattery
- Confident without overselling — state what you did, not how "passionate" you are
- Never beg, apologize for reaching out, or hedge with phrases like "I know you
  are very busy"

## LinkedIn Connection Note Rules
# Connection notes are capped at 300 characters (hard LinkedIn limit).
# The Drafter must produce a note that fits — no truncation allowed.
- Open with a specific hook: shared school, shared project domain, their recent
  post, or their exact team/program (e.g. "saw your work on 787 empennage
  structures")
- State your identity in one clause: "MS AE at UIUC, composites background"
- End with a low-friction CTA: "Would value connecting" or "Happy to connect"
  — never ask for a call in the connection note
- No hashtags, no emojis, no exclamation marks
- Target 200-270 characters; leave buffer for the 300-char limit

## LinkedIn Post-Connection Rules
# Sent after the connection is accepted, typically within 24-48 hours.
- One short paragraph, 3-5 sentences max
- Remind them briefly who you are (one sentence)
- Mention one specific thing about their work or company that is genuinely
  relevant to your background — do not fake it; leave a [RESEARCH_NEEDED]
  placeholder if the agent lacks that information
- State what you are looking for clearly and without apology
- CTA should be conversational: offer a 15-minute call or ask if they know
  the right person to talk to — not both
- Do not attach a resume in this message; offer to share it if they ask

## Cold Email Rules
# [CUSTOMIZE] Adjust word limits and CTA style to match your preference.
- Subject line: 6 words or fewer; specific, not clever; examples:
    "Structural composites background — open to FT roles"
    "UIUC AE student — interested in [Team/Program name]"
  Avoid: "Quick question", "Exciting opportunity", clickbait subject lines
- Total word count: 100-150 words for the body; never exceed 175
- Paragraph 1 (2 sentences): who you are + the one most relevant credential
- Paragraph 2 (2-3 sentences): why this company/team specifically; cite
  something real (program name, material they use, recent news)
- Paragraph 3 (1-2 sentences): clear ask — informational call, referral to
  recruiter, or awareness of open roles; pick one, not all three
- No bullet points in cold emails — prose only
- No attachments in first contact; offer to send resume if they reply
